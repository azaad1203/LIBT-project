A model fit for purpose

What are the different scopes ?
  • Business use case
    • Primary actor:Customer
    • Goal:To get some goods they need
    • Scope:widget warehouse business
    • Main sequence:
      1. Customer rings up with order
      2. Warehouse dispatches the goods
      3. Customer signs the delivery note
      4. Warehouse sends an invoice
      5. Customer sends back a cheque
  • system use case
    • Primary actor: Sales Clerk
    • Goal: To take a telephone order
    • Scope: Sales Transaction IS
    • Main Sequence:
      1. Enter Customer number – IS displays Customer data
      2. Enter Item number – IS displays Item data
      3. Enter quantity required – IS calculates price & VAT
      4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 for more items
      5. At end – IS displays total & possible delivery dates
      6. Select a delivery date – IS confirms order taken
who are the actors and stakeholders ?
  • Stakeholder: Something or someone with a vested interest in the behaviour or outcome of the use case
    • May never appear in the action sequence
    • “Company”, “Board”, “Regulators” …
  • Actor: A stakeholder who interacts with the system during the Use Case
    • Roles vs. Job Titles
    • Primary Actor: who starts it off
    • Supporting Actor: required external support
What are conditions and consequences ?
  • Trigger: The event that initiates the use case.
    • What causes the actor to start it.
  • Pre-conditions: What must be true before the use case can occur.
    • Assumption about the state of the world.
    • Things that have happened already.
  • Guarantees: What must be true after the use case has occurred.
    • Success Guarantee: if the case succeeds.
    • Minimum Guarantee: however the use case ends.
What are Extensions ?
  • possible variations in the step sequence.
  • How to recover when things happen differently.

Is the analysis or design right?
  • There is no one right model for a system.
  • The chances of two analysts drawing the exact same model are very small.
  • Many possible models can be made – all fit for purpose.
Is it "fit for purpose"?
  • Are all the documents correct?
    • Correct template for level
    • All parts filled in
  • Are they consistent?
    • One view cannot refer to stakeholders, actions, or information which conflicts with another view.
    • Behaviour must be consistent in every relevant view.
    • key questions
      1. Are the entries on different parts of the Use Case template internally consistent?
      2. For any output (i.e. to support a decision) is there a use case that provides the relevant data?
  • Will it lead on to a technically "good" solution?
    • Reliable – low number of errors
    • Easy to build
    • Easy to maintain
    • The design is clear and easy to follow
    • Good modularity
  • Is it a recognisable description of the system being analysed?
    • Do all the names used reflect elements of the real world problem area?
    • Do they come within the statement of purpose?
    • Is there any obvious vocabulary from the problem area missing
  • Does it provide all the necessary information?
    • Definition A
      • A collection of interacting computer programs that supply Information to human Decision Makers.
    • Definition B
      • A collection of interacting computer(s) and humans that makes management decisions within an organisation.
What is a Statement of Purpose & Scope ?
  • A short concise statement of the primary purpose, function or role of the system being developed.
  • A touchstone for What is IN the System.
  • May say explicitly What is NOT included.
What determines information quality ?
  • Given the purpose of the system are its outputs:
    • Appropriate
    • Accurate
    • Brief
    • Precise
    • Timely
    • Up-to-Date
